What is Theta Tau?

Theta Tau is the oldest, largest, and foremost Fraternity for Engineers. The purpose of Theta Tau is to develop and maintain a high standard of professional interest among its members, and to unite them in a strong bond of fraternal fellowship. The Theta Epsilon chapter at New York University was established on November 19, 2016 by thirteen founders. At NYU, we focus on achieving our goals within the three pillars of our fraternity: Brotherhood, Profession, and Service.


Brotherhood Professionalism Service

While Theta Tau focuses on cultivating professionalism amongst its members, we carry a large emphasis on unity and camaraderie to create memorable experiences during our time at NYU . Our Brotherhood chairs have organized events where brothers can strengthen their bonds of friendship and create meaningful memories to look back on in the future. A few of the many events we have held in the past include potlucks, game nights, alumni dinners and excursions off-campus to hold team building activities.

As a professional engineering fraternity, our main goal is to provide resources and connections for our members to use in establishing their career in whatever path they choose to take. Here at Theta Tau, we keep moving forward to whatever opportunities are before us, and we strive to help each other reach our own personal goals. In the previous semesters, we have hosted several information sessions, resume workshops, and alumni panels to benefit not only our members, but to everyone in our engineering community.

While it is important to focus on our personal growth, Theta Tau believes that it is just as crucial to give back to the community that we reside in. This not only provides us new experiences to learn about, but also raises awareness of the greater social issues that can be found in our city. In the past, we have participated in volunteering opportunities such as packing lunch bags for the homeless, cleaning up local parks, and assembling blankets for animals in shelters.

Requirements
  • • You must attend at least 1 General Interest Meeting and 2 other events in order to be considered for a bid.
  • • You must be enrolled in an Engineering program (Includes Applied Physics, Biomolecular Science, Business Technology and Management, Computer Science, and Construction Management)
  • • You must have a current cumulative GPA of 2.5 or above and be in good academic standing
  • • You must be at least 18 years old at the time of initation
